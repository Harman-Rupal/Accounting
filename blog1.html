BDO (an acronym for Binder Dijker Otte) is an international network of public accounting, tax, consulting
and business advisory firms that provide professional services under the name BDO. It is the fifth-largest accounting network in the world. Global fee income of the member firms in the network for the year ended
30 September 2023, including the members of their exclusive alliances, totaled US$ 14 billion.[3] Each BDO member firm is an independent legal entity in its own country. In 1973, the organisation adopted the name BDO, made up from the initials of the three founding firms: Binder (UK), Dijker
(Netherlands) and Otte (Germany).

# What is the difference between Big 4 and BDO? I'm talking mainly about the business side of the company.

The difference between Big 4 and BDO is mainly related to the size, scope, and reputation of the firms. The Big 4 are the four largest accounting firms in the world, with revenues ranging from $34.64 billion to $59.3 billion in 20231. They have offices in over 150 countries and employ hundreds of thousands of professionals. They offer a wide range of services, such as auditing, tax advisory, consulting, and financial advisory, to clients across various industries and sectors. They are known for their high-quality standards, global reach, and expertise in complex and challenging projects.

The main advantages of working for the Big 4 are: Exposure to diverse and prestigious clients, such as multinational corporations, governments, and NGOs. Opportunity to work on large-scale and high-impact projects that require multidisciplinary teams and innovative solutions. Access to extensive resources, training, and career development programs that enhance professional skills and knowledge. Possibility to travel and work internationally, and to network with global colleagues and experts. The main disadvantages of working for the Big 4 are: High pressure and workload, with long hours and tight deadlines. High competition and performance expectations, with constant evaluation and feedback. High turnover and burnout rates, especially among junior staff. Limited autonomy and creativity, due to strict policies and procedures.

The main advantages of working for BDO are: Exposure to diverse and entrepreneurial clients, such as family businesses, start-ups, and social enterprises. Opportunity to work on smaller-scale and more personalized projects that require close collaboration and communication with clients. Access to more flexible and supportive work environment, with more autonomy and empowerment. Possibility to develop deeper relationships and trust with clients and colleagues. The main disadvantages of working for BDO are: Lower revenue and market share, compared to the Big 4. Limited global presence and recognition, especially in emerging markets. Fewer resources, training, and career development opportunities, compared to the Big 4. Lower compensation and benefits, compared to the Big 4.
